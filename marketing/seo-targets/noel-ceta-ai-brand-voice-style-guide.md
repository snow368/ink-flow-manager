## 3. Full thread (verbatim capture)

### Root
AI-generated content sounds generic. Because it is. ChatGPT has no idea what your brand voice sounds like. I built style guides for 18 agencies using AI. Those with brand-enforced style guides: 3.4x higher quality scores. Here's the system: 🧵

### 1/ The AI style guide problem
Default AI output: "This comprehensive guide explores the multifaceted aspects of digital marketing strategy." Your brand voice: "Here's why most marketing strategies fail (and how to fix yours)." Same topic. Different voice. AI needs explicit instructions.

### 2/ The 4-component style guide (for AI)
- Component 1: Tone & personality (how do we sound?)
- Component 2: Language rules (what we say, what we don't)
- Component 3: Structure templates (how do we organize?)
- Component 4: Brand references (examples of good/bad)

### 3/ Component 1: Tone & personality
Define your brand voice: "We're direct, no-BS, occasionally funny. We avoid corporate jargon."
AI prompt: "Write in a direct, conversational tone. Avoid corporate language. Use 'we' not 'the organization.' Include occasional irreverent humor."
Examples matter: "Good: 'This is broken.' Bad: 'This may require optimization.'"

### 4/ Component 2: Language rules
Words you USE: client, customer, agency, framework; direct words (fix, not optimize); questions (engage readers).
Words you DON'T use: "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm"; passive voice; corporate fluff phrases.
AI prompt: "Use these terms: [list]. Avoid these: [list]. Prefer active voice. Use questions to engage."

### 5/ Component 3: Structure templates
Blog post structure: 1. Hook (provocative statement) 2. Problem (specific to reader) 3. Framework (4-5 steps) 4. Real example (case study) 5. CTA (specific action).
AI prompt: "Use this structure: Hook → Problem → Framework → Example → CTA. Nothing else."
Constraints = consistency.

### 6/ Component 4: Brand references
Good examples: "This framework increased revenue by 340%. Here's how..." / "You're probably making this mistake..." / "The data shows X. Most people think Y."
Bad examples: "It is widely believed that..." / "This cutting-edge solution offers..."

### 7/ Real case: SaaS agency
Before style guide: AI content generic/corporate; client feedback "doesn't sound like us"; manual rewrites 40%.
After style guide: brand-consistent; "this is us"; manual rewrites 8%.

### 8/ Testing your style guide
Generate 10 pieces with the guide. Score each: Tone consistency (1-10), Language adherence (1-10), Structure compliance (1-10), Overall brand fit (1-10). Target 8+ on all. If <8: refine guide, regenerate.

### 9/ Closing
AI content needs guardrails. Build a style guide. Enforce it in prompts. Test for consistency. Your brand voice stays yours. AI becomes a tool, not the voice.
