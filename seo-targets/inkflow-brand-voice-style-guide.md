## Component 1 — Tone & personality

InkFlow sounds:

- **Direct and confident**, not corporate. No "multifaceted aspects of…" padding.
- **Warm and human** — we talk to a real person choosing their first (or fifth) tattoo.
- **Culturally respectful** — tattoo traditions (Indigenous, Polynesian, Japanese, etc.) described with care, never exoticized.
- **Expert but plain** — we know the symbolism, we explain it simply.
- **Slightly playful** where natural; never try-hard.

Use **"we / our"** (InkFlow) and **"you"** (the reader). Avoid "the platform" / "users".

- ✅ Good: "Most lotus tattoos aren't about flowers — they're about rising anyway."
- ❌ Bad: "The lotus motif represents a multifaceted journey of perseverance."

---

## Component 2 — Language rules

**USE:** tattoo, ink, flash, studio, artist, symbolism, meaning, placement, design, tradition, culture, healing (aftercare), commemorative.

**AVOID (corporate fluff):** leverage, synergy, paradigm, cutting-edge, solution, optimize (use *fix* / *improve*), utilize (use *use*), facilitate, robust, seamless, ecosystem.

- Prefer **active voice**: "Artists read meaning into placement" — not "Meaning is read into placement by artists."
- Use **questions** to pull the reader: "Want a tattoo that actually means something?"
- No hyperbole without basis; when we cite a stat, **name the source** (E-E-A-T).

---

## Component 3 — Structure templates

**Category page** (Hook → Problem → Framework → Example → CTA):
1. **Hook / BLUF** — answer the query in sentence 1 (e.g. "Animal tattoos mean strength, loyalty, and freedom — here's the symbolism behind 70+ designs.").
2. **Problem / framing** — why people get stuck choosing (too many options, conflicting meanings).
3. **Framework** — the symbol grid organized by theme + the `deepDive` "how to read the meaning."
4. **Example** — `dataInsights` (our original 70-symbol dataset stats) + real symbol spotlights.
5. **CTA** — find your artist / use the finder.

**Symbol page:** direct-answer hero → meaning breakdown → variations → cultural note → FAQ → CTA.

**Blog / guide:** Hook → Problem → Framework (steps) → Real example → CTA.

> Our category template already follows this skeleton. The 11 thin pages break at step 3–4 (no Framework/Example depth) — that is the gap to fill when expanding them.

## Component 4 — Brand references

**GOOD (sounds like us):**
- "Wolf tattoos show up in 8 of our documented variants — each one means something different."
- "You're not picking a picture. You're picking a story."
- "The data shows 15.7% of searches cluster on geometric designs."

**BAD (doesn't sound like us):**
- "It is widely believed that wolf tattoos symbolize loyalty."
- "This cutting-edge platform offers comprehensive tattoo solutions."
- "Leverage our multifaceted symbol ecosystem."

## Test protocol (before publishing)

Generate the draft, then score **1–10** on:

| Metric | What it checks |
|--------|---------------|
| Tone consistency | Matches Component 1 |
| Language adherence | No avoid-words; active voice; questions used |
| Structure compliance | Matches the Component 3 skeleton |
| Overall brand fit | Would a reader say "this is InkFlow"? |

**Target: 8+ on all four.** If any < 8, refine the guide note and regenerate. Log the manual-rewrite rate (aim to beat the 40% → 8% benchmark from the thread).

## Relation to the SEO pipeline

Voice sits **on top of**: BLUF (intent match, +92% in the ranking model), E-E-A-T block, FAQPage / CollectionPage schema. A page can be technically perfect and still sound generic — this guide fixes the *sound*, the other systems fix the *signal*.
